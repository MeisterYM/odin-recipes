Cottage Pie

Description

Meat and vegetable filled pie topped with mashed potatoes.

Difference between this and shepherd's pie is the meat.

Real shepherd's pie uses lamb.

If other ground meat is used, it is considered cottage pie.

Inspired by this guide.

Ingredients

  • 1 lbs ground beef
  • 1 onion diced
  • 2 carrots 1/2 inch diced
  • 2 ribs celery 1/2 inch diced
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 2 Tbsp flour
  • 1 1/2 cups beef broth
  • 2 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 Tbsp dried parsley
  • 1/4 tsp dried thyme leaves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2/3 cup peas
  • 1/4 tsp salt and pepper each
  • 2 lbs russet potatoes or Yukon gold potatoes
  • 3 Tbsp melted butter
  • 1/3 cup warm milk
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar shredded (optional)

Steps for Cooking

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F.
  2. Peel potatoes and cut into 1-inch chunks.
  3. Place chunks in large pot of cold and salted water.
  4. Bring potatoes to gentle boil and cook for 13-16 minutes until fork tender.
  5. While potatoes are cooking, combine ground beef and onion in large skillet.
  6. Brown ground beef until no pink remains.
  7. Add carrots, celery, and garlic to ground beef mixture.
  8. Cook mixture for additional 5 minutes or until tender.
  9. Stir in flour and cook for 1 minute.
  10. Stir in beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, tomato paste, parsley, thyme, and bay leaf.
  11. Simmer for 20 minutes or until gravy has thickened.
  12. Remove bay leaf and replace with peas while seasoning with salt and pepper.
  13. Drain potatoes and mash until smooth.
  14. Stir in butter while adding warm milk a little at a time until potatoes are smooth and fluffy (might not need all of the milk).
  15. Top mixture with potatoes and place in oven.
  16. Bake 20-25 minutes until golden.
