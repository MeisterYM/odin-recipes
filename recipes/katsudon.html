Katsudon

Description

Japanese style dish.

Based on cutlets and eggs over rice.

Name comes from a combination of the words "tonkatsu" and "donburi".

Inspired by this guide.

Ingredients

  • 2 center-cut boneless pork chops (pounded down to 1-inch thick)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Flour for dusting
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup panko
  • Oil for frying
  • 1/2 cup dashi stock or chicken stock
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp Mirin
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 servings steamed white rice
  • 1 scallion

Steps for Cooking

  1. Season pork chops with salt and pepper.
  2. Dust with light coating of flour.
  3. Beat egg in a bowl.
  4. Add thin, even layer of oil to a cast iron pan or skillet over medium heat.
  5. Dip pork into egg for coating.
  6. Transfer to panko for next coating.
  7. Lay pork chops in hot oil and cook for 5-6 minutes on one side until it becomes golden brown.
  8. Flip pork chops over and cook other side for same amount of time.
  9. Drain pork chops on a plate with paper towel.
  10. While pork is resting, add stock, sugar, soy sauce, and Mirin into a small bowl.
  11. In separate bowl, beat 2 eggs.
  12. Add tablespoon of oil to a pan over medium heat.
  13. Add sliced onion to pan.
  14. Fry onions until they are translucent and slightly caramelized.
  15. Pour stock mixture over the onions.
  16. Slice tonkotsu into pieces and place on top of onions.
  17. Drizzle egg over everything.
  18. Cook over medium low heat until egg is set.
  19. Serve over bowls of white rice and garnish with scallions.
